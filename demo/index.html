🚆 Tempo
perps batch auction — how the dual auction works
The idea: instead of matching trades the instant they arrive (fast bots win), Tempo collects orders for a short window and matches them at one fair price. It runs two auctions at once — a BID auction (makers buy × takers sell) and an ASK auction (takers buy × makers sell) — so makers never trade makers and takers never trade takers.

🔬 Under the hood — dual find_cross
find_cross (ported faithfully from program/src/clearing.rs): two-pass — pass 1 finds V = max·min(D,S); pass 2 picks the lowest plateau tick where neither side's strictly-better quantity exceeds V, so settlement conserves (Σ buy fills = Σ sell fills = V). Run once per auction (bid + ask).
